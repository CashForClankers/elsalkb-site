The Natural Wonder

El Salvador's Hidden Underwater World

Los Cóbanos is the largest coral reef between Mexico and Costa Rica — a 20,000-hectare marine sanctuary and Ramsar Wetland of International Importance. For thousands of miles in either direction, it's the only substantial hard-substrate reef habitat on the Pacific coast.

Unlike Caribbean limestone reefs, Los Cóbanos is a volcanic reef — formed over ancient lava flows, creating a rugged underwater landscape of caverns, arches, and coral gardens unique in the Americas. Below the surface: the 19th-century shipwrecks Sheredos and Douglas, the first underwater archaeological sites in Central America.

📋 What to Know Before You Go

🧴Biodegradable sunscreen only — required to protect the coral. We sell it on-site.
👟Water shoes — the volcanic reef is sharp and rocky. Essential for getting in and out.
🌊Snorkel at low tide — best visibility at Playa El Amor and La Privada.
📅Book 48 hrs ahead — especially during Semana Santa and peak whale season (Jan–Feb).
🏖Golden sand — the beach is made of crushed coral and shells, not the volcanic black sand typical of El Salvador.
⛺Nearby: El Imposible National Park (1.5 hrs inland) for intensive forest hiking after your reef day.
